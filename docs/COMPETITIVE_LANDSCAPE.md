# Competitive Landscape

This document is a positioning note, not a source of implementation truth.

## Strategic Read

The relevant market lesson remains the same:

SoundForge should compete on workflow quality for developers, not on the broad claim that AI audio exists.

The strongest positioning is still:

**Prompt-to-pack game audio for developers, with cleanup and automation built in.**

## Categories That Matter

### Browser-first AI SFX tools

These validate demand for:
- fast prompt-to-sound workflows
- downloadable variations
- game-oriented audio generation

### General AI audio platforms

These validate:
- prompt-based SFX generation as a mainstream expectation
- duration and looping controls as real product requirements

### Open and self-hostable models

These matter because SoundForge benefits from preserving a local or self-hosted path.

### Procedural SFX tools

These remain strategically relevant for retro and highly deterministic effects.

## Implication For SoundForge

The repo should continue to lean into:
- CLI-first workflows
- deterministic local integration
- manifests and pack generation
- backend portability
- game-engine orientation

It should avoid trying to become:
- a general audio studio
- a browser-polished SaaS competitor
- a full music and voice platform in early releases
